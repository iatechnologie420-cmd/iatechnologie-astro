<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="The_need_to_secure_your_computer"></span>The need to secure your computer<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>In the current era, where activities are becoming digital at an accelerated pace, the security of our computer has become a major concern for individuals as well as businesses. Here is a detailed guide on the good reflexes to adopt to effectively protect your computer system.</p>



<p>Computers are the central hubs of our personal and professional data. They are also gateways to access an unlimited amount of information and services online. However, this openness to the world can expose users to numerous risks such as malware, phishing, hacking and even identity theft. Protecting your computer has therefore become essential to preserve not only the confidentiality, but also the integrity and availability of our information.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Lets_start_by_understanding_the_different_threats"></span>Let&#8217;s start by understanding the different threats<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>The first step toward better security is understanding the types of threats that exist. Malware includes viruses, Trojans, spyware and ransomware (<strong>malware</strong>) The most common. Cybercriminals may also use social engineering techniques like phishing to trick users into obtaining sensitive information.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Install_a_reliable_antivirus"></span>Install a reliable antivirus<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>It is crucial to equip your computer with quality antivirus software. This type of program is specially designed to detect, block and remove malware. Opt for recognized solutions like <strong>Norton</strong>, <strong>McAfee</strong>, or <strong>Kaspersky</strong>, and be sure to keep your antivirus up to date for optimal protection.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Keep_the_operating_system_and_software_up_to_date"></span>Keep the operating system and software up to date<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Software updates are not only intended to provide new features, they also fix security vulnerabilities. Stay vigilant and regularly install the latest updates for your operating system and the software you use.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Strengthen_network_security"></span>Strengthen network security<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>At home or at work, the network your computer is connected to must be secure. This includes using strong passwords for your Wi-Fi and using VPNs (<strong>Virtual Private Network</strong>) to encrypt your internet traffic when you are connected to public networks.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Adopt_secure_browsing_practices"></span>Adopt secure browsing practices<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Stay careful when browsing the internet. Avoid clicking on suspicious links, don&#8217;t download files from unverified sources, and always be alert for phishing attempts. Using dedicated security browser extensions can also provide you with an extra layer of protection.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Data_backup"></span>Data backup<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Despite all preventative measures, no system is foolproof. This is why it is essential to regularly back up your important data to external media or cloud services. In the event of a computer attack, you will be able to recover your information.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Automatic_screen_lock"></span>Automatic screen lock<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>A simple but effective option to secure your computer is to set the automatic screen lock. This can be done through the computer&#8217;s sleep settings.</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>Below <strong>Windows</strong>, you can go to System Settings and then Screen Saver to set a time after which the screen automatically locks.</li>



<li>For users of <strong>MacOS</strong>, this option can be found in System Preferences, under Screen Saver or Security &#038; Privacy where you can adjust the auto-lock settings.</li>
</ol>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Manual_locking_methods"></span>Manual locking methods<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Manual locking is a method of controlling when exactly your screen locks.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Lock_your_PC_in_Windows"></span>Lock your PC in Windows<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>        On an operating system <strong>Windows</strong>, the most common keyboard shortcut to lock the screen is:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Windows + L</strong> : This combination instantly locks your session.</li>
</ul>



<p>        If you use <strong>Windows 10</strong> or later versions, you can also use the functionality <strong>Dynamic Lock</strong> which automatically locks your PC when you walk away with a paired Bluetooth device.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Lock_Your_Mac"></span>Lock Your Mac<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>        For users of a <strong>Mac</strong> with macOS, locking can be done via different methods:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Control + Command + Q</strong> : Allows you to quickly lock the screen.</li>



<li>Or use the key combination <strong>Option-Command-Eject</strong> Or <strong>Option + Command + Power button</strong> on newer Macs.</li>
</ul>



<p>        Furthermore, in the <strong>System Preferences</strong> You can set options so that the screen automatically locks after a period of inactivity.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="On_Linux_Operating_Systems"></span>On Linux Operating Systems<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>        If you are a user of <strong>Linux</strong>, the shortcut varies depending on the desktop environment:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>For <strong>Ubuntu</strong> or GNOME, use <strong>Super + L</strong> (the Super key is often symbolized by the Windows logo on keyboards).</li>



<li>Below <strong>KDE</strong>, use <strong>Ctrl+Alt+L</strong>.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Use_of_strong_password"></span>Use of strong password<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>A strong password is your first line of defense against unauthorized access.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Create a password that mixes upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols.</li>



<li>Avoid using easily guessable personal information.</li>



<li>Do not reuse a password already used on other accounts or services.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Multi-Factor_Authentication_AMF_or_2FA"></span>Multi-Factor Authentication (AMF or 2FA)<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>For enhanced security, enable multi-factor authentication. This requires additional proof that you are the owner of the account.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>This could be a confirmation on your mobile phone or the use of a physical security key.</li>



<li>So much <strong>Windows</strong> that <strong>MacOS</strong> support various forms of MFA to discourage unauthorized access attempts.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Biometric_Security"></span>Biometric Security<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Biometric technology uses your fingerprint or face as keys to access your system.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Modern computers from brands like <strong>Microsoft</strong> with Windows Hello or <strong>Apple</strong> with Touch ID and Face ID offer these biometric security options.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Security_software_and_regular_updates"></span>Security software and regular updates<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Installing reliable security software and performing regular updates to your operating system and applications is crucial.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Make sure you have good antivirus and anti-malware software.</li>



<li>Perform regular updates to fix security vulnerabilities.</li>
</ul>



<p>Securing your <strong>PC</strong> Or <strong>Mac</strong> is an essential step to protect your personal and professional data. It is recommended to combine multiple locking methods and remain vigilant about the strength of your passwords and system updates. By following these tips, you will be able to effectively lock your computer against unwanted intrusion.</p>
