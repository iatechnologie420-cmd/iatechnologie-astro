<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Identifying_a_compromised_Instagram_account"></span>Identifying a compromised Instagram account<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>With the constant increase in users on social platforms like <strong>Instagram</strong>, the risks of account hacking have intensified. Know how to identify if your account <strong>Instagram</strong> has been compromised is essential to be able to act quickly and effectively.</p>



<p>In this article, let’s learn about the warning signs of a compromised account and the steps you can take to recover it.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Signs_of_a_Compromised_Instagram_Account"></span>Signs of a Compromised Instagram Account<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>There are several warning signs that should alert you to the security of your account <strong>Instagram</strong>. Here are the most common:</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Suspicious_account_activity"></span>Suspicious account activity<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Posts, messages or comments that you did not make.</li>



<li>Unexpected change to your username, bio, or contact information.</li>



<li>Subscriptions to unknown or unwanted accounts.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Unusual_connection_alerts"></span>Unusual connection alerts<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Notifications from <strong>Instagram</strong> regarding connections from unknown devices or locations.</li>



<li>Emails from<strong>Instagram</strong> indicating password changes or other security changes that you did not initiate.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Unable_to_access_your_account"></span>Unable to access your account<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Login failed despite using the correct password.</li>



<li>Received an error message stating that your account does not exist.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Contact_by_third_parties_indicating_unusual_behavior"></span>Contact by third parties indicating unusual behavior<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Friends or followers informing you of friend requests or messages that they consider suspicious and that you did not send.</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Actions_to_take_if_your_account_is_compromised"></span>Actions to take if your account is compromised<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Change_your_password_immediately"></span>Change your password immediately<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>If you can still access your account, the first line of defense is to change your password to a strong, unique password. Make sure it contains a combination of letters, numbers and symbols.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Use_the_two-factor_security_feature"></span>Use the two-factor security feature<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Enable two-step verification offered by <strong>Instagram</strong> to add an extra layer of security. So even if someone gets your password, they won’t be able to access your account without the two-factor security code.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Contact_Instagram_Support"></span>Contact Instagram Support<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>If you cannot reset your password because the email address or phone number associated with your account has changed, contact Support<strong>Instagram</strong> to report a compromised account.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Inform_your_subscribers"></span>Inform your subscribers<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Once you regain control of your account, inform your subscribers that your account has been hacked. This will help clarify any suspicious activity they may have noticed.</p>



<p>Vigilance is required on social networks and securing your accounts must be a priority. By paying attention to warning signs and taking appropriate action quickly, you can minimize account damage <strong>Instagram</strong> compromise and ensure the security of your online presence.</p>

<p>Losing access to a social media account can be a frustrating and stressful experience. Fortunately, there are immediate methods you can try to recover your account. Now let’s look at clear and concise solutions to regain access to your account safely.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Using_the_Service_Recovery_Features"></span>Using the Service Recovery Features<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>Most of <strong>social networks</strong> offer account recovery options in the event of a hack or loss of access. Follow these generic steps:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Go to the login page for the relevant service.</li>



<li>Click on the “Forgot your password?” link. » or “Need help? “.</li>



<li>Enter your email address or phone number associated with your account.</li>



<li>Follow the instructions sent by email or SMS to reset your password.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Contact_technical_support"></span>Contact technical support<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>If recovery via classic options does not work, it may be necessary to contact the social network’s technical support. Keep all relevant information about your account handy to make the process easier.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Go to the help or support section of the site.</li>



<li>Use the contact forms to explain your situation.</li>



<li>Add any proof of account ownership (screenshots, previous transactions, etc.).</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Identity_verification"></span>Identity verification<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Some platforms require identity verification to prove that you are the account owner. Be prepared to send a copy of your government-issued ID.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Provide a clear image of your ID.</li>



<li>Make sure all information is legible and matches the account details.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Use_of_third-party_tools"></span>Use of third-party tools<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>There are third-party tools that specialize in account recovery. Be very careful with these services and only opt for those with a reliable reputation.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Look for reviews and feedback on the tool being considered.</li>



<li>Check that the tool is recommended by cybersecurity experts.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Prevention_and_safety"></span>Prevention and safety<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Once your account is recovered, take steps to secure your access:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Change your password to a complex and unique combination.</li>



<li>Enable two-factor authentication if available.</li>



<li>Review the permissions granted to third-party applications.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Securing_Instagram_account_after_recovery"></span>Securing Instagram account after recovery<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="1792" height="1024" src="https://pub-5bb2cd26c04a41efbcb7bf8a165f9044.r2.dev/images/blog/Comment-recuperer-un-compte-Instagram-pirate-1-1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1625" srcset="https://pub-5bb2cd26c04a41efbcb7bf8a165f9044.r2.dev/images/blog/Comment-recuperer-un-compte-Instagram-pirate-1-1.png 1792w, /images/blog/Comment-recuperer-un-compte-Instagram-pirate-1-1-300x171.png 300w, /images/blog/Comment-recuperer-un-compte-Instagram-pirate-1-1-1024x585.png 1024w, /images/blog/Comment-recuperer-un-compte-Instagram-pirate-1-1-150x86.png 150w, /images/blog/Comment-recuperer-un-compte-Instagram-pirate-1-1-768x439.png 768w, /images/blog/Comment-recuperer-un-compte-Instagram-pirate-1-1-1536x878.png 1536w" sizes="(max-width: 1792px) 100vw, 1792px" /></figure>



<p>Recovering an account <strong>Instagram</strong> hacked is a big relief, but the security of your account remains a top priority to avoid a recurrence. Here are the essential steps to secure your Instagram account after recovering it.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Change_password"></span>Change password<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>As soon as your account is recovered, change your password immediately. Choose a password that is strong, unique and difficult to guess. It is advisable to use a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers and symbols.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Enable_two-factor_authentication"></span>Enable two-factor authentication<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>L’<strong>two-factor authentication</strong> (A2F) adds an extra layer of security to your account. With 2FA enabled, you will need your password and a one-time code sent to your phone to log in.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Check_account_information"></span>Check account information<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Review and update your profile information, such as the email address and phone number associated with your account. Make sure they are yours and that they are secure.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Scan_connected_third-party_apps"></span>Scan connected third-party apps<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Revoke access from third-party apps that are suspicious or that you no longer use. Third-party apps can sometimes be a hole in your account security <strong>Instagram</strong>.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Inform_your_subscribers-2"></span>Inform your subscribers<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Warn your followers about the previous situation and ask them to ignore suspicious messages that might have been sent from your account when it was compromised. This will help prevent the spread of malicious links or scams.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Use_a_password_manager"></span>Use a password manager<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>To avoid reusing the same password across different services, consider using a <strong>password manager</strong>. This will help you create and store distinct and complex passwords for all your online accounts.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Monitor_account_activity_regularly"></span>Monitor account activity regularly<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Keep an eye on your login activity. If you notice suspicious logins, act immediately to secure your account.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Update_the_app_regularly"></span>Update the app regularly<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Make sure you always have the latest version of the app <strong>Instagram</strong>. Updates often contain important security fixes.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Avoid_connections_on_public_or_shared_devices"></span>Avoid connections on public or shared devices<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Avoid signing into your account on public or shared devices, as they can be compromised and put your account security at risk.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Be_vigilant_against_phishing"></span>Be vigilant against phishing<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Be wary of suspicious emails or messages asking for your login information. <strong>Instagram</strong> will never ask you for your password in an email or direct message.</p>



<p>By following these steps, you will strengthen the security of your Instagram account and significantly reduce the risks of another hack. Cybersecurity is an ongoing commitment; Always stay ahead of new security practices and industry updates.</p>
