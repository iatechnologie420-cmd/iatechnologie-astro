<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Diagnosing_your_computers_breakdown"></span>Diagnosing your computer&#8217;s breakdown<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Encountering a computer problem can sometimes turn into a real headache. The computer is a complex technological compound, and when something doesn&#8217;t work as expected, there are often a multitude of possible reasons. In this article, we offer you a practical guide to help you identify the problem and find possible solutions for diagnosing your computer&#8217;s failure.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Identification_of_warning_signs"></span>Identification of warning signs<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>The first step in diagnosing a computer failure is to spot the symptoms. Common indicators include:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>System errors or blue screens (BSOD)</li>



<li>A computer that restarts randomly</li>



<li>Overheating of the machine</li>



<li>Extreme slowness issues</li>



<li>Abnormal noises coming from the central unit</li>



<li>Problems connecting to the Internet or devices</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Hardware_component_analysis"></span>Hardware component analysis<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Hardware failures are often the cause of computer malfunctions. Carefully examine the following components:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>The hard drive :</strong> check its health with software like CrystalDiskInfo.</li>



<li><strong>RAM:</strong> use tools like MemTest86 to detect memory errors.</li>



<li><strong>The graphics card:</strong> Visual artifacts or crashes in games may signal a problem.</li>



<li><strong>Power supply:</strong> A PC that suddenly shuts down may indicate a faulty power supply.</li>



<li><strong>Fans:</strong> make sure they are working properly to avoid overheating.</li>



<li><strong>Ports and cables:</strong> Malfunctions can sometimes be due to simple false contacts.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Checking_the_operating_system_and_software"></span>Checking the operating system and software<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>A corrupted operating system or malware can also cause problems. Check the following:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Perform a <strong>virus scan</strong> to detect possible malware.</li>



<li>Update the operating system and all drivers.</li>



<li>Use the System Restore feature if the problem started recently.</li>



<li>Consider reinstalling the operating system as a last resort.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Viewing_event_logs"></span>Viewing event logs<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>Windows event logs contain valuable information that can guide fault diagnosis. To get there :</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Press on <strong>Windows + X</strong> and select Computer Management.</li>



<li>Click Event Viewer > Windows Logs.</li>



<li>Look for critical errors and warnings around the time the problem occurred.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Contact_a_professional"></span>Contact a professional<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>If you have not been able to identify or resolve the problem, it may be a good idea to contact a professional. A qualified technician will be equipped to make a thorough diagnosis and make any necessary repairs.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Troubleshooting_software_and_operating_system_issues"></span>Troubleshooting software and operating system issues<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Now let&#8217;s focus on software and operating system issues. These problems can range from minor inconveniences to major malfunctions that paralyze your daily activities.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Understanding_the_operating_system"></span>Understanding the operating system<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p>Before diving into troubleshooting, it is essential to understand what a <strong>operating system</strong> (SE). The OS is the major software that manages the hardware and provides common services for application software. Popular operating systems include <strong>Microsoft Windows</strong>, <strong>macOS</strong>, <strong>Linux</strong>, And <strong>Android</strong>. Knowing which OS you are working on is the starting point for any troubleshooting.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Identifying_software_problems"></span>Identifying software problems<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>It is important to know how to identify if a problem is caused by software. Symptoms such as applications behaving unexpectedly, repeated crashes, or frequent errors when opening are indicators. Some OS tools, such as <strong>Task Manager</strong> on Windows or <strong>Activity monitor</strong> on macOS, allow you to monitor software performance and identify possible problems.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Common_operating_system_problems"></span>Common operating system problems<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>The OS can also be the source of inconveniences: slow startup, blue screens of death (BSOD) on Windows or the colored wheel of death on macOS, or even updates that do not install correctly. These issues often require actions such as booting into safe mode, using diagnostic tools, or performing a system restore.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Basic_solutions_for_software_problems"></span>Basic solutions for software problems<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>To troubleshoot software issues, start with simple steps: </p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Check your software updates and install them.</li>



<li>Restart the application or OS.</li>



<li>Use the OS&#8217;s built-in repair or diagnostic functions.</li>



<li>Uninstall and reinstall the problematic software.</li>



<li>Check online forums or the software&#8217;s technical support site for specific solutions.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Use_advanced_tools"></span>Use advanced tools<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>When basic solutions are not enough, you have to turn to advanced tools:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Restoring the OS to an earlier date when everything was working normally.</li>



<li>Use third-party diagnostic and repair software.</li>



<li>Go to the command line for more specific repairs (including PowerShell on Windows or Terminal on macOS and Linux).</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Safeguarding_and_prevention"></span>Safeguarding and prevention<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>The best troubleshooting is the one you don&#8217;t have to do. Regularly implement prevention measures, such as:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Create OS restore points.</li>



<li>Perform backups of important data.</li>



<li>Keep the OS and software up to date.</li>



<li>Use a good antivirus and antimalware.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="When_to_call_a_professional"></span>When to call a professional<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h4>



<p>If after all these steps the problem persists, it might be wise to consult a professional. More complex problems like severe virus infections, critical system errors, or hardware failures often require specific skills and tools that specialized computer troubleshooting technicians possess.</p>



<p>In conclusion, although software and OS issues can be intimidating, there are many tools and resources available to help resolve them. By following the steps methodically, normal operation can often be restored without having to call a specialist.</p>
